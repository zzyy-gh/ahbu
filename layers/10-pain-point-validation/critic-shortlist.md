# Critic pass — selection-shortlist.md — 2026-05-02

## Verdict

**pass-with-fixes**

The shortlist is a substantive piece of work that does not commit selection (correctly — selection is layer 10's downstream step) and does name its convergent finding plainly. But several recommendations rest on hidden assumptions, the "deferred" calls on cross-subject-eeg and affective-state are weaker than the prose admits, and the feasibility framing for the GTX 1650 is overstated for at least one top candidate. The next step (`selection.md`) must close these before locking.

## Findings

### Critical

- **critical — Premature deferral of `cross-subject-eeg` on "redundancy" grounds.** — `selection-shortlist.md` §"Recommendation for selection debate" line 41. — The shortlist defers cross-subject-eeg because "unique angle is 'evaluation diagnostic on foundation models' which is valuable but narrow and already partly addressed by EEG-FM-Bench (arXiv:2508.17742)." That contradicts what the underlying candidate file actually surfaced: EEG-FM-Bench *flags* the subject-dependent-split inflation problem but the candidate (cross-subject-eeg.md §3c) cites the EEG Foundation Models critical review (arXiv:2507.11783) explicitly stating "the universality and robustness of EEG-FMs have not been convincingly demonstrated in most studies" and "4 of 10 surveyed models evaluated on data already used during pre-training." The same candidate file (§6) lays out a quality-bar program — subject- + dataset- + hardware-disjoint splits, 0/1/5/20-shot curves, per-subject distributions, pre-training-overlap audit — that is *not* what EEG-FM-Bench did. The shortlist conflates "field has named the gap" with "field has filled the gap." Fix: in `selection.md`, either rebut the candidate's own §6 program, or undo the deferral.

- **critical — "GTX 1650 fits" is hand-waved for ecg-ppg-realworld at the framing the shortlist proposes.** — `selection-shortlist.md` row "Feasibility @ GTX 1650" (col ecg-ppg-realworld) and Recommendation #1. — The shortlist proposes "calibrated abstention for AFib alerts on PTB-XL + a PPG corpus" or "skin-tone-stratified PPG eval." The PTB-XL + classical-ML + abstention path is genuinely 4 GB-feasible. The PPG-corpus path is only feasible if the "PPG corpus" is small (PPG-DaLiA, BUT-PPG, WESAD-style); the shortlist does not name which corpus, and the underlying candidate (ecg-ppg-realworld.md §5) names PPG-DaLiA, WESAD, MAUS, BUT-PPG without saying which is the operative one. More importantly, the **skin-tone-stratified eval** framing is feasibility-blocked by a constraint the candidate itself flagged (ecg-ppg-realworld.md §5 risk 3, §7 Q3): public ECG/PPG corpora largely lack skin-tone labels. The shortlist promotes this scope without acknowledging the data-availability blocker that the underlying candidate explicitly raised. Fix: `selection.md` must name the operative dataset(s) for skin-tone eval and confirm Fitzpatrick-or-equivalent labels are present, or drop that framing.

- **critical — Negative-result defensibility on skin-tone framing is asserted, not argued.** — `selection-shortlist.md` "Selection debate must" bullet 4 line 50. — The shortlist *asks* whether scope is defensible if results are negative ("a negative skin-tone-disparity result, or a negative abstention-improvement result, still has to be a valuable contribution") but does not *answer* the question for either framing. A negative skin-tone-disparity result on a small public corpus is a near-null contribution if (a) the corpus has weak/proxy skin-tone labels, (b) sample size in the darker-pigmentation stratum is too small to power a non-inferiority claim, or (c) the JMIR meta-analysis already established the effect at population scale. The shortlist names this as a question for selection but treats it as procedural. It is load-bearing.

### Major

- **major — Hidden assumption: "narrow ecg-ppg to abstention/skin-tone" presumes calibrated-abstention work is novel.** — Recommendation #1 line 38. — The recommendation rests on "abstention angle distinctive" (comparison row "Risk of redundancy"). No citation supports that claim. The PhysioNet/CinC 2020 paper itself (cited in candidate §3g) reports a ~10 % drop on hidden test data, which is the standard motivating story for selective-classification work in cardiology. The shortlist needs to spend at least one paragraph of `selection.md` checking the abstention/selective-classification literature on ECG/PPG (Geifman & El-Yaniv selective classification, conformal-prediction-on-ECG work, Strodthoff et al.'s PTB-XL benchmark uncertainty discussion in arXiv:2004.13701 which the candidate already flagged) before committing this framing.

- **major — `affective-state` deferral on "redundancy" overweights one paper.** — `selection-shortlist.md` line 43, comparison row "Risk of redundancy" col affective-state. — The shortlist tags affective-state as "High" redundancy because "Brookshire 2024 + Apicella 2024 already named the leakage gap." The underlying candidate (affective-state.md §3 "Reproducibility of physiological features") cites a result that those two papers do *not* deliver: arXiv:2508.10561 finds "only 2 EDA features" of 164 reproducible across studies — a finding strong enough to motivate a feature-stability-audit contribution that is not what Brookshire (DNN-EEG leakage) or Apicella (cross-subject review) delivered. The shortlist's redundancy verdict is correct on leakage-only framings but wrong on the broader affective-state candidate. Fix: either narrow the "redundancy" claim to leakage-only, or surface the feature-reproducibility angle as a non-redundant alternative scope.

- **major — Reachable-constituency claim is not verified for the named top candidates.** — `selection-shortlist.md` "Selection debate must" bullet 3 ("Verify the constituency is reachable for feedback (not just citable)"). — The shortlist names this as a future-work item but does not perform the check. For ecg-ppg-realworld, the candidate (§7 Q1) explicitly says "I could not directly verify specific Reddit thread URLs ... within this research pass" and (§7 Q5) "the Apple / Fitbit alerting algorithms are closed; a contribution that improves them in principle may not be adoptable in practice." For sleep-staging, the candidate (§7) says "Need primary-source clinician interviews to disambiguate." Both candidates have **unreached constituencies**, and the shortlist promotes them anyway. Fix: `selection.md` must show evidence of at least one outreach-or-attempt-to-outreach (forum post, GitHub issue, email to dataset maintainer, MOABB/braindecode issue) before the methodology layer locks scope.

- **major — Sleep-staging feasibility leans on datasets whose license-compatibility is asserted.** — `selection-shortlist.md` row "Data access friction" col sleep-staging line 23 ("Sleep-EDF + Dreem-DOD + HMC PSG (no DUA)"). — Sleep-EDF on PhysioNet is genuinely no-DUA (Open Data Commons). HMC PSG on PhysioNet 2018 is similarly accessible. Dreem-DOD-H/-O are released by Dreem under a CC-BY-4.0-ish license but historically required a separate signup; the shortlist treats them as "no DUA" which is a *practical* claim (no formal DUA) but glosses over registration friction. This is minor for sleep-staging itself but the row's "Medium-high" framing for sleep already acknowledges the NSRR DUA lag — the shortlist should be consistent and flag Dreem-DOD registration friction at the same granularity. Fix: state the registration step explicitly in `selection.md`.

- **major — "Convergent finding" overstates independence.** — `selection-shortlist.md` §"Convergent finding across candidates" lines 8–13. — The convergence ("evaluation hygiene") across three of four candidates is real, but the candidates were generated by the **same researcher in the same pass** with awareness of the GTX 1650 constraint. The "convergence" is partly a selection effect of one researcher reasoning about what fits 4 GB. Treating it as *evidence of pain shape* rather than *evidence of one researcher's framing* is a category error. Fix: in `selection.md`, distinguish "the pain has a hygiene-shaped contribution available" (true, evidenced) from "the field is asking for evaluation hygiene" (only partially evidenced, mainly via Brookshire 2024 + Apicella 2024 + Chevallier 2024 + the EEG-FM critical review).

### Minor

- **minor — Time-to-first-honest-result column has no methodology behind the numbers.** — `selection-shortlist.md` row "Time-to-first-honest-result" line 27. — "4–8 weeks", "3–6 weeks", "4–10 weeks", "3–6 weeks" are presented without derivation. They are plausible but unsourced. Either drop the row or briefly note the basis (e.g., "weeks of focused effort by single researcher per candidate's §5"). The risk is that a downstream reader uses these as estimates.

- **minor — "Label-grounded > construct-contested" claim is asserted.** — `selection-shortlist.md` §"Cross-cutting observation" lines 30–32. — "For the AHBU quality bar, label-grounded variants are stronger" is presented as obvious. It is defensible (and consistent with vision §"Hard constraints" honesty bar) but is doing real work in the deferral of affective-state. State the argument explicitly: weak labels make falsifiability harder, which collides with the quality bar — *that* is the link, not "label-grounded is better."

- **minor — "EEG cross-subject and affective-state share the same root pain" undercuts the Comparison table's per-row distinctions.** — Line 32 vs comparison rows 19–28. — If the root pain is shared, then "Pain real (evidence strength)" High vs Mixed and "Risk of redundancy" Crowded vs High should be reconciled. Either the candidates are different pain points (the table's framing) or the same pain point with different framings (the cross-cutting observation). Pick one for `selection.md`.
